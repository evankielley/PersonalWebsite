IceDEF

The aim of this project is to develop a model that predicts the path that an iceberg will take. Many such models have been made in the past but none have managed to make predictions that were accurate enough on a short time-scale. To differentiate our model from the models of the past, we use climatogical data from numerous models (not just one) to get a better representation of input field uncertainty. The main components that our model will require as input are: wind and ocean current velocities, SSTs, and initial coordinates, dimensions, and time. We hope that with accurate data from the fields just mentioned that we will be able to make accurate predictions; however, the project is still in the early stages.

Internal Wave Experiment

This project focused on studying the physical properties of internal waves as they propagate along a stratified channel. The experiment itself was performed at MUN in a wave tank equipped with an internal wave generator. The analysis of the experiment was done using a technique known as synthetic schlieren in order to visualize waves that were nearly invisible to the naked eye. The results we obtained provided insight into the relationship between stratification and the properties of the wave however no papers were published on our findings. Future work for the project does exist though since the experiment can be modified to explore how the waves reflect off boundaries of varying slopes. This is an area that is light on previous research and has practical applications for those modeling waves as they encounter coastal topography.
